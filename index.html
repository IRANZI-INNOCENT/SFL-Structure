Introduction to SFL

What is the Scholars League?

A dedicated soccer tournament designed exclusively for the Mastercard Foundation Scholars community at USIU-Africa, creating a powerful platform for community building and skills development beyond academics.

Key Components

  • 16 teams representing "families" named after iconic historical figures

  • Competitive tournament format with group stages and knockout rounds

  • Multiple participation roles: players, coaches, representatives, and supporters

The League's Purpose

Beyond the Game

Creating a lasting tradition that enhances the overall Scholars' experience

Academic Connection

Complementing academic pursuits with practical leadership opportunities

Vision Statement

"To establish the Scholars League as a highlight of the Mastercard Foundation Scholars program, creating a sense of unity and healthy competition."

Team Composition

Each family will form and register one soccer team

Teams will have a roster size of between 11 and 25 players

Tournament Format

Group Stage

  • 16 teams divided into 4 groups (A, B, C, D) of 4 teams each

  • Each team plays every other team in their group twice (home and away)

  • 6 group matches per team in total

  • Top 2 teams from each group advance to the knockout stage

Knockout Stage

  • Quarterfinals and Semifinals played over two legs (home and away)

  • Winner determined by aggregate score across both matches

  • If tied after two legs: Extra time, then penalty shootout if needed

  • Final is a single match played at a neutral venue

  • Away goals rule is not applied

Roles and Participation

Everyone Has a Part to Play

The Scholars League encourages participation in various capacities

Players

Actively competing on the field for their family teams

  • Represent their family on the pitch

  • Develop teamwork and leadership skills

  • Maintain physical fitness and active lifestyle

Coaches

Guiding team strategy and managing players

  • Develop gameplay strategies and formations

  • Manage team dynamics and player development

  • Enhance tactical understanding of the game

Family Representatives

Leading the organization and management of their teams

  • Handle team registration and administration

  • Coordinate with league organizers and other teams

  • Promote team spirit and family identity

Fans and Supporters

Following the league and cheering on teams

  • Attend matches and cheer for their family teams

  • Help promote and spread the word about the league

  • Create a vibrant and supportive atmosphere
